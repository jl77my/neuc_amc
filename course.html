Course


Synopsis

The NEUC Australian Mathematics Competition (AMC) course offers an in-depth and challenging preparation for high school students who want to excel in mathematics. It integrates theoretical learning with hands-on problem-solving activities to build a thorough understanding of mathematical concepts and improve students' problem-solving skills.

Program Overview:

  • Objective: Equip students with a solid foundation in mathematics and prepare them for the AMC by developing analytical and problem-solving skills.
  • Approach: The course features engaging lessons, interactive problem sets and mock tests to challenge students and build their confidence.
  • Benefits: Students will improve their mathematical proficiency, preparing them for the AMC and other academic challenges.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will have achieved the following outcomes:

  • In-Depth Understanding of Key Concepts:
    • Algebra: Mastery of algebraic expressions, equations, inequalities, and functions. Ability to solve complex algebraic problems and apply these skills to real-world scenarios.
    • Geometry: Comprehensive knowledge of geometric principles, including shapes, theorems, proofs, and spatial reasoning.
    • Statistics and Probability: Proficiency in analyzing data, understanding statistical measures, and applying probability concepts to various problems.
    • Number Theory: Insight into fundamental number theory concepts, including prime numbers, divisibility rules, and modular arithmetic.
  • Enhanced Problem-Solving Skills: Ability to approach and solve complex mathematical problems using multiple strategies and techniques. Development of critical thinking and logical reasoning skills necessary for tackling challenging questions.
  • Preparation for Competitive Exams: Familiarity with the format and types of questions typically found in the AMC and similar competitions. Improved test-taking strategies and time management skills through practice exams and simulated tests.
  • Confidence in Mathematical Abilities: Increased confidence in solving advanced mathematical problems and participating in mathematical competitions. Greater enthusiasm and interest in mathematics, leading to potential further studies in STEM fields.

Syllabus

The course syllabus is designed to cover essential mathematical topics in a structured and efficient manner over 6-8 weeks.

Week 1-2: Algebraic Expressions and Equations

  • Topics Covered: Simplification, solving linear and quadratic equations, inequalities, and systems of equations.
  • Activities: Problem sets and real-life applications of algebra.

Week 3-4: Geometry

  • Topics Covered: Geometric shapes, angles, properties of triangles and polygons, and coordinate geometry.
  • Activities: Geometric constructions and problem-solving exercises.

Week 5: Statistics and Probability

  • Topics Covered: Descriptive statistics, probability rules, and data interpretation.
  • Activities: Analyzing data sets and solving probability problems.

Week 6: Number Theory

  • Topics Covered: Prime numbers, greatest common divisors, and modular arithmetic.
  • Activities: Number theory puzzles and exploration of number patterns.

Week 7-8: Advanced Topics and Mock Exams

  • Topics Covered: Advanced problem-solving techniques and review of key concepts.
  • Activities: Comprehensive review sessions and practice exams.

Assessment

Assessment methods are designed to evaluate understanding and track progress throughout the course.

Weekly Quizzes:

  • Purpose: Assess weekly topics and provide immediate feedback.
  • Format: Short quizzes with multiple-choice and open-ended questions.

Practice Exams:

  • Purpose: Simulate the AMC environment and test problem-solving abilities.
  • Format: Full-length practice exams with AMC-style questions.

Interactive Activities:

  • Purpose: Reinforce learning through engaging exercises.
  • Format: Group sessions and mathematical games.

Instructor Feedback:

  • Purpose: Provide personalized guidance and improvement recommendations.
  • Format: Feedback on quizzes, exams, and participation.
